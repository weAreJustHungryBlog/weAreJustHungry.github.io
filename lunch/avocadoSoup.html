Avocado Soup


Do you ever get the feeling during a hot summer day that you’re hungry but don’t really feel like eating a proper meal as it would be too hot and too heavy? This Avocado soup recipe is a perfect option for such a day as it is a light meal option and you can eat it chilled which will help to refresh yourself.

We have adjusted the recipe a bit to our liking, the original recipe came from the Food Network

Ingredients:


  • Olive oil
  • 1 cup diced white onion
  • 1 serrano Chile, stemmed, seeded, and diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper for seasoning
  • firm ripe avocados
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • ¼ cup fresh lemon juice
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 cups water
  • Pitta Bread
  • Chickpeas
  • Salt, pepper, paprika powder

Preparation:

  1. Heat some olive oil in a medium skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion, Chile and the garlic and cook until slightly soft.
  3. Season with salt, to taste. Remove from the heat and set aside to cool.
  4. Put the avocados in a large bowl and add the chicken broth, lemon juice, cilantro, onion mixture and water.
  5. Add, in batches, to a blender and puree until smooth, straining each batch of pure into a large bowl.
  6. Stir in some salt and pepper, to taste.
  7. Cover and refrigerate until chilled.

To accompany the soup, we added roasted chickpeas, cashews and some crème fraiche. We also prepared pitta bread chips sprinkled with Paprika to dip in the soup.

Chickpeas:

Cover a baking tray with precooked chickpeas and add salt, pepper and paprika powder. Put them in the oven at about 200 degrees Celsius and let them roast until they become crunchy.

Pitta Chips:

Cut pitta breads into triangles and separate the two layers. Cover up a baking tray with the pitta chips and cover with oil, salt, pepper and paprika powder. Put them in the oven at about 175 degrees Celsius until they become a little bit brown and crunchy. For a healthier option, use full wheat pitta bread.
